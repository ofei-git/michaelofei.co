The title

Lead Editor | Semrush

Chosen deliberately. "Lead" means in the arena, setting direction by doing the hardest version of the work. "Editor" — from the Latin edere, to put forth — means the person who decides what gets said and how. Understated by design. The title doesn't need to claim expertise. The work does that.

The title is elastic. Lead Editor at $96k running a blog is one thing. Lead Editor at $144k shaping the AI search narrative for Adobe+Semrush is another. Same title, vastly different scope. It doesn't need to change — the work underneath it does.

The shift

This isn't a title change. It's an identity change — and a mode change. For two years I pushed myself into SOPs, team coaching, editorial operations. I was good at it. But Leigh saw something I didn't: the highest-leverage thing I do isn't managing the system. It's the judgment calls on the court.

Lead Editor in the arena, shaping how the market thinks about AI search
Words clarify the thinking. The thinking shapes the narrative. The narrative influences the market.

The field of AI search is being defined right now. The protocols are shipping, the infrastructure is being rebuilt, and the market is looking for sound guidance. There are maybe a dozen people globally positioned to become the reference voices in this space. I'm one of them — not because I know the most, but because I have the access, the platform, the research infrastructure, and the ability to out-teach everyone else.

The stakes

Adobe is deferring to Semrush to lead on agentic search positioning. Not co-lead. Not "align with Adobe's direction." Lead. Andrew shared an internal letter with the Adobe team and they're fully on board.

Think about what that means. The content I'm writing isn't Semrush blog content. It's shaping how one of the world's largest creative software companies talks about the next era of online visibility. The audience isn't just Semrush users — it's the combined Adobe+Semrush market. Millions of marketers, creatives, and businesses.

The AI Search OS is on Semrush's CEO's radar. Adobe is bought in. The mandate is ~3 content assets per week on agentic search. This isn't a side project. This is the strategic priority of a company being acquired for billions.

Every foundational article I write now becomes the reference point for how this combined entity talks about AI search. That's not a content assignment. That's building institutional knowledge.
