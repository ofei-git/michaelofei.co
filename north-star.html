North Star

How I spend my time. Why it matters.
Last updated: June 2026
I help marketers create content worth recommending: to customers, to their teams, and to the machines now deciding who gets seen.

The point

Every piece of work I make is trying to earn a recommendation I didn't ask for, from someone willing to spend their own reputation to pass it on.

That is the measure. Not views, not polish, not how good I think it is. Your brand is what they say it is.

I am a communicator, not an artist. The artist serves the work. I serve the recommendation, and craft is how I get there. Everything below follows from that.

It matters more in the AI era. The thing I want a person to do, recommend me and cite me, is exactly what I want AI systems to do. Earned recommendation is the currency of the whole shift I'm writing about. It's both what I write about and how I measure myself.

What content is

Content is communication, deliberately shaped into an asset, built to move a specific room.

It almost always starts the same way: writing, or talking it out loud. From there it becomes whatever artifact best meets the room. A memo. A spreadsheet. A mocked-up landing page. A deck. An article. A video. The format is a decision I make on purpose, and the writing and the thinking sit underneath all of it.

It serves two rooms, and I refuse to rank them.

External
The market. Customers, prospects, the wider industry.
Internal
The business. The team, leadership, the people I need to move to get something built.

A Loom for five colleagues and a YouTube video for fifty thousand are the same job. So are a board memo and a thought-leadership article. Different rooms, same craft, same test: did it earn the remark.

The ladder of evidence

Earned recommendation comes in grades, a ladder from cheap and common at the bottom to rare and valuable at the top.

rarer · higher value ↑
Recommendation named
A decision-maker or an AI system citing me directly. Rarest, highest.
Endorsement credibility
Unprompted shares, detailed comments, inbound. A person spending their credibility on me.
Resonance
Did it hold them. Looks different per asset: watch time on a video, reading time on an article, whether a memo got acted on.
Reach
Views, impressions. Did it get distributed.

I want views. Distribution primes the pump, and nothing resonates that nobody sees. I just refuse to let the bottom rung pretend to be the whole ladder.

And I keep two questions apart: did it get a fair test, and did it resonate. Something unseen hasn't failed to land. It hasn't been tested yet.

Excellence, defined

Excellence here means resonance. Obsessively understanding what keeps this audience up at night, what is relevant to them, what is relatable, and adapting the story so it moves them while I'm still being myself. Comedy if it takes comedy. A plain memo if it takes a plain memo.

Read flat, even a great story is boring. The room decides whether you earned its attention, so you meet the room.

Sometimes the job is to align with the room, sometimes to challenge it, sometimes to delight it. Sometimes the right move is intentionally rough, more like Screaming Frog than a polished brochure, because rough is what earns trust for that audience. For every packaging decision I'm asking one thing: what experience meets this room.

The stakes

Adobe is deferring to Semrush to lead on agentic search positioning. Not co-lead. Lead.

The content I write shapes how one of the world's largest software companies talks to its market about the next era of visibility. The AI Search OS is on the CEO's radar. This is a rare window where the work shapes an industry in real time. Seize it.

The gap: consistency

I have the depth. I have the storytelling. I have a fast feedback loop for improving.

The missing term in the equation is consistency, and consistency is really focus.

I ship a lot, so volume isn't the problem. The problem is spread: too many directions, too large an appetite. Consistency means doing the few things that compound, repeatedly, in public, long enough for the recommendation to stack.

The weekly question that keeps me honest: what am I shipping, to which room, to earn which remark.
