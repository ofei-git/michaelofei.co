Concept 4: The Method in Different Industries

B2B — Manufacturing
"How Predictive Maintenance Cut Our Downtime by 40%"
Writer
Sarah Chen
Editor, Industrial Equipment Weekly
Thesis: Predictive maintenance ROI depends more on culture change than technology. Interviewed 3 plant managers, identified the pattern.
Expert
Marcus Webb
Plant Manager, Midwest Automotive Parts
18 months implementing predictive maintenance. Real numbers on downtime reduction, team resistance, what actually changed.
Contributor
Dr. Anil Kapoor
Industrial IoT Consultant
Technical context on sensor technology, common implementation mistakes, validates Marcus's approach.
Why authority stays with Sarah
Marcus knows what he did. Sarah identified that his success came from culture change, not just technology—a pattern she saw across all three interviews.
B2C — Fitness
"Your First Marathon: What the Training Plans Don't Tell You"
Writer
James Okonkwo
Editor, Runner's Path Magazine
Thesis: The mental game matters more than the physical after week 12. Structures the piece around the emotional arc, not just the training blocks.
Expert
Coach Diana Reyes
USATF Certified Coach, 20 years experience
Training methodology, common mistakes she sees, how to know if you're overtraining, race day strategy.
Contributor
Tom Nakamura
First-time marathoner, finished Chicago 2025
The reader's perspective. What surprised him, where he almost quit, what he wishes he'd known.
Why authority stays with James
Diana has the expertise, Tom has the relatable experience. James saw that both kept returning to the same insight about the mental shift after week 12—that's his pattern.
B2B — Healthcare IT
"EHR Migration: Lessons from 3 Hospital Systems"
Writer
Rachel Oduya
Senior Editor, HealthTech Today
Thesis: Successful EHR migrations prioritize physician workflow over feature completeness. Synthesized across 3 CTO interviews.
Expert
Dr. Michael Torres
CTO, Regional Medical Center (450 beds)
Led 18-month Epic migration. Specific data on adoption rates, physician satisfaction scores, what went wrong in month 6.
Contributor
Linda Park, RN
Nurse Informaticist, same hospital
Front-line perspective on what the CTO's decisions looked like in practice. The gap between leadership vision and daily reality.
Why authority stays with Rachel
Torres knows his migration. Rachel identified that all three CTOs—despite different systems and sizes—made the same mistake and recovered the same way.
B2C — Home & Garden
"Starting a Vegetable Garden: A Year-One Survival Guide"
Writer

Thesis: Year-one success is about soil and expectations, not seed selection. Frames the piece around managing disappointment, not just technique.

What she got wrong year one, what she'd do differently, the emotional rollercoaster of watching plants die.
Why authority stays with Emma

Concept 5: The Ownership Test

Expert owns it
Writer owns it
Guest post: Expert writes, you publish
Expert's piece
Q&A interview: Expert answers, you transcribe
Mostly expert
Quote roundup: Multiple experts, thin synthesis
Dispersed
Lens Method: Expert proof, writer's pattern
Writer's piece
Original research: Your data, your conclusions
Fully writer
The Test
Could the expert have written this piece themselves? If yes, you're a conduit. If no—because you brought a frame, a synthesis, an audience understanding they don't have—you're the writer.
